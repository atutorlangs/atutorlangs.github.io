  • 2012-10-12 21:57:59

    Re: Flowplayer problem

    I have not looked into this, because I don't want to use Flash. The reason being that Flash does not work in iPad and iPhone, which doesn't support Flash.

    That being said, I am inclided to *think* that the most correct thing to say is that there is a bug in ATutor. And my suspicision is that it is a byte serving bug:

    http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Byte_serving

    The reason why I asked about Safari was because Safari is basically incompatible with ATutor when it comes to serving audio and video *without* Flash. Other browsers than Safari does not require byte serving - at least ont the way Safari requires it - so they don't have problems.

    As for ATutor plus Flash, then I don't know if the byte serving issue is the problem. May be I am just too preoccupied with "byte serving" so that I see byte serving problems everywhere. But nevertheless, my suspicision is that this *is* the reason for your problem.

    The Apache Web server handles byte serving, aparently. But when files are served via PHP, as is the case with ATtutor, then the byte serving side of things needs to be implemented in PHP.

    I have reported a bug about this: http://atutor.ca/view/3/23505/1.html
    (But note that I did not know what "byte serving" was when I filed that bug, so, as you can see, that bug started out as something completely different.)
