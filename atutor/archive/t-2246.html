    Easy Success

    For what its worth, I have a copy of ATutor 1.4.1 running as a PostNuke module on my baseline site at http://www.dnlt.com.

    I haven't populated it, as of this moment, but suffice it to say that it was a piece of cake, except for the sleuthing required to find the updated pnATutor file that CAS had posted in the forums.

    The site is hosted via services provided by MidPhase in Chicago. Before moving it to my "live" site, I prototyped it using VMware on my XP laptop with RedHat 9 and Apache 2.0.40. I know the directions/suggestions don't recommend Apache 2.x, but I did NOT have any problems making it work.

    I have also added the ACollab in my VMware environment, and I may move that to the live site as well, although for the moment, I'm trying to figure out how to best use them in a semi-production environment.

    If you need assistance or want to trade ideas, please post and we'll pick up off-line or continue the threads here. I would be VERY interested in assisting an organization that would like to use ATutor, et al in real life in the U.S.

    I prefer to work for $$$ but if the story is good, I could be available for on-site consulting for the cost of travel and living expenses.

    This posting is based on Greg's encouragement to share the "out-of-the-box" experience.
