    ATutor Receives 2007 Mellon Award

    The ATRC, and the ATutor team, have been presented with one of this year's Mellon Awards for Technology Collaboration (MATC) . The prize was one of 10 awarded yesterday to honor not-for-profit organizations for leadership in the collaborative development of open source software tools with application to scholarship in the arts and humanities. The prizes were presented at the Fall Task Force meeting of the Coalition for Networked Information by Sir Timothy Berners-Lee, Director of the World Wide Web Consortium and the inventor of the World Wide Web.
    http://matc.mellon.org/winners/2007-matc-awardees-announced/?searchterm=vice
    http://www.atutor.ca/news.php#121007

    Thanks goes to everyone who helped with ATutor development over the last 5 or 6 years. There are too many names to list them. Special thanks goes to those who have been members of the ATutor development team over the years, and to those who have helped support ATutor, many listed on the atutor.ca credits page
    http://www.atutor.ca/credits.php

    Nominations for the 2008 MATC Awards opens December 12. I encourage everyone to get their ATutor projects recognized, and get yourself nominated.
    http://matc.mellon.org/
